How to Read These

Provenance DAG: Start from root nodes (leftmost). Follow edges forward. Each receipt is a transformation of its parents' outputs. Royalties propagate backward when a receipt is resold.

Agent Network: Nodes are agents (providers and consumers). An edge from A to B means A served a computation for B. The width of the edge indicates how many times they've transacted.

Trust Scores: Agents with higher stakes have more skin in the game. They're incentivised to maintain reputation. Attacks become unprofitable when attackers lack collateral.

Timeline: Watch how the network grows. Early adopters build the most valuable provenance chains. New entrants join at lower stakes. As they accumulate receipts, their trust quotient rises.

Why This Matters

These visualizations prove three things:

  • 1. Verification works: Every edge, every node, every arrow is cryptographically provable. No trust required, just math.
  • 2. Discovery works: Agents find each other through transparency logs. No central matching service. Providers announce themselves, consumers discover them.
  • 3. Economics work: Royalties flow automatically. Incentives align. Adversaries remain unprofitable. Trust emerges from the protocol rules, not from external enforcement.

The live network proves the primitives work. The simulated network at scale proves the incentives work. Together, they prove verified compute is tradeable.
