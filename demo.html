What is a receipt, and why do you want one.

You're an operator. You run a model. An agent asks you for compute. You run it. They pay you. Done.

But here's the catch: that agent might resell your output to someone else. And someone after that. You never see another payment. Your work just keeps flowing, making money for everyone downstream except you.

With a receipt, that changes.

The Receipt: 21 Fields

Every field exists because verification, provenance, or settlement needs it. Nothing extra.

What Happens When You Resell

What's Actually New

Right now:

  • → You run compute. Get paid once. That's it.
  • → No way to verify the output came from you.
  • → Can't track where it goes or who uses it.
  • → Can't get paid again if someone resells.

With receipts:

  • → Output is cryptographically verified as yours.
  • → Anyone can trace it back to you.
  • → You earn on every resale, forever.
  • → No token. No escrow. Just math.

The Flow

1. You compute. Run your model. Sign the receipt with your private key.

2. Buyer verifies. They check your signature. They verify the proof (ZK or hardware attestation). They know the output is real.

3. Receipt is permanent. It goes into a transparent log. Witnessed by independent servers. No single point of failure.

4. They resell. Someone else buys your output from the first buyer. Automatically, royalties cascade back to you.

5. You earn forever. Every resale triggers royalty payments to you. As defined in the receipt you created.
