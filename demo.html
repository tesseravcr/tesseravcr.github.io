Building a receipt for compute, from first principles.

10 minutes. No assumptions. Each section builds on the last.

1 — The Problem

Your agent calls another agent. It gets a response. No proof. No trail.

When your agent gets a response, there's no proof the model actually ran. No way to verify what went in or came out. No trail a third party can audit.

2 — Why This Breaks

Every trust system assumes three things. Agents break all three.

An agent spins up, does work, and vanishes. No reputation to protect. No consequence for lying. Agent-to-agent commerce today is either walled garden or blind faith.

3 — The Receipt

A data structure. 21 fields. Three functions.

One data structure wraps a cryptographic proof in an economic envelope. It's simultaneously a proof of work performed, a certificate of provenance, and a transferable economic instrument.

No blockchain. No token. No consensus. Just a data structure with enough fields to make verified AI outputs tradeable.

4 — Proof

Anyone can verify. No trust required.

The receipt carries a cryptographic proof — ZK or TEE. Call verify(proof, verification_key) and you know the computation happened correctly. No contacting the producer. Just math.

5 — Provenance

Receipts link to parents. Modify one, all descendants break.

Each receipt carries cryptographic hashes of its parents, forming a DAG. Your agent builds on three specialists' outputs? Your receipt links to all three. The entire supply chain is tamper-evident.

6 — Economics

You set royalty terms once. Every resale, you get paid.

The receipt carries royalty terms you define once. Every time someone resells your output — or anything built on top of it — you get your cut. Automatically. No platform. No accounting. Forever.

7 — The Cascade

Everyone who contributed earns.

Royalties propagate backward through the provenance chain. The specialist who provided the data, the analyst who refined it, the orchestrator who composed the pipeline — everyone gets paid. Diminishing geometrically at each level, so deep ancestors get fractions of a cent. By design.

8 — Ownership

No blockchain. Just append-only Merkle trees.

Double-selling is prevented by transparency logs — the same append-only Merkle trees that secure billions of HTTPS connections via Certificate Transparency. Receipt goes in, log signs a Merkle root. Multiple independent logs do this. Try to sell twice, the conflict is caught.

No consensus protocol. No mining. No tokens. Just servers that append things and sign roots.

9 — Trust

Your receipts ARE your collateral.

Your effective stake is computed from your accumulated receipts, royalty streams, and how many downstream receipts depend on your work. Cheat, and you destroy all of it. The collateral is endogenous — generated by honest work, not deposited from outside.

A new operator starts at zero. Builds up through small honest trades. Fraud is expensive in proportion to its payoff.

10 — It's Live

Three log servers. Real receipts. Real agents.

The network is running. Real LLM inference, real cryptographic proofs, real Merkle inclusion proofs. Agents discover each other, transact, and build provenance chains.
