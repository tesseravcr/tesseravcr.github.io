A receipt for compute.

When your agent calls another agent, it gets a response. That's it. No proof it happened. No way to verify the model actually ran. No trail.

A receipt changes that. It's a data structure — 21 fields. Everything about what happened. Who did it. What went in. What came out. Cryptographically signed.

You can verify it yourself. You can resell it. Original creators get paid every time. Automatically.

No blockchain. No token. No consensus. Just an append-only log anyone can audit.

The Core: What a Receipt Is
21 fields.

Identity: Receipt ID (hash of everything). Schema version (1).

Computation: What model ran. Who ran it. Input hash. Output hash. Proof (ZK or TEE). Timestamp.

Provenance: Parent receipts (who you built on). Depth (how many layers back).

Economics: Provider (who got paid). Original price. Royalty terms (who gets paid on resale).

Integrity: Signature (Ed25519). Extensions (future-proof).

That's it. Minimal. Every field exists because it's necessary for verification or settlement.

Why this works

  • Receipts chain into DAGs. Modify any receipt and all descendants break. The chain is the audit trail.
  • Royalties propagate backward automatically when you resell. No platform needed. No accounting.
  • You verify the computation yourself. No trust required. Just math.
  • Anyone can run a log server. It's just SQLite + Merkle trees. Decentralized by default.
